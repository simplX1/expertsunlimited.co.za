Adam Phillips

Adam studied Business Studies at the University of Plymouth, England. His third year of his course was spent in industry and Adam joined L Messel and Company(part of Shearson Lehman at the time). He managed to have an incredible year, working in New York as well as London. He then went back to finish his degree and his dissertation was centred around purchasing power parity. Adam then joined Kleinwort Benson three weeks before the 1987 crash. It was here that he took an interest in currencies, although most of his time was spent on the equity desk. Adam moved to South Africa in 1993 and under took a MBA at UCT. He worked for Caltex Oil, managing their foreign exchange crude oil position before moving back into banking with the Board of Executors. He stayed with BoE until 2002 before moving to Investec's Treasury Division. Adam then set up Umkhulu Treasury (Pty) Ltd in 2010. He uses his experience in all matters of treasury to assist a variety of companies. He is also a tax practitioner and assists with tax/foreign exchange matters. Adam also assists attorneys and advocates with exchange control advice and has been involved in a number of cases in relation to the topic.
